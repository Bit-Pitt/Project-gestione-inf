Murder, She Said

Year: 1961

Genre: comedy film

Country: United Kingdom

Plot: Murder She said is a 1961 comedy/murder mystery film directed by George Pollock, based on the 1957 novel 4.50 from Paddington by Agatha Christie. The production stars Margaret Rutherford as Miss Marple, along with Arthur Kennedy, Muriel Pavlow, James Robertson Justice, and Stringer Davis (Rutherford's husband). MGM made three sequels, Murder at the Gallop, Murder Most Foul and Murder Ahoy!, all with Rutherford starring as Miss Marple. She also cameoed as Marple in MGM's The Alphabet Murders, which starred Tony Randall as Hercule Poirot.
