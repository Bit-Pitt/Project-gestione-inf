Young Noszty and Mary Toth

Year: 1960

Genre: comedy film

Country: Hungary

Plot: Young Noszty and Mary Toth (Hungarian: A Noszty fiú esete Tóth Marival) is a 1938 Hungarian-German romantic comedy film directed by Steve Sekely and starring Pál Jávor, Éva Szörényi and Erika von Thellmann. It is based on the 1909 novel of the same title by Kálmán Mikszáth. It was shot at the Hunnia Studios in Budapest. The film's sets were designed by the art director Márton Vincze. It was screened at the Venice Film Festival that year. A separate German-language version Ihr Leibhusar was also produced. The novel was later remade into a 1960 film Young Noszty and Mary Toth.
