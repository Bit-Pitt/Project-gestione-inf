The Last Stage

Year: 1947

Genre: drama film

Country: Poland

Plot: The Last Stage (Polish: Ostatni etap) is a 1948 Polish historical drama film directed and co-written by Wanda Jakubowska, depicting her experiences in the Auschwitz concentration camp during World War II. The film was one of the early cinematic efforts to describe the Holocaust. In film criticism, it is often referred to as "the mother of all holocaust films". It was Jakubowska's first theatrically-released film and was both a commercial and critical success.
